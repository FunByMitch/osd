Matching Game

Flip tiles. Find pairs. Watch them vanish in a blaze of glory.

What is Matching Game?

Configurable board

Set any width (2–12) and height (2–12) using scroll wheels. Up to 144 tiles (12×12) with 15 shapes × 10 colors = 150 unique pairs.

Timed scoring

The clock starts on your first flip. Finish faster and in fewer moves for a better result — time and moves shown on the win screen.

Fireworks + vortex

Matching a pair triggers a death-spiral vortex and an explosive fireworks burst in the tile's own color palette.

Hint system

Hint briefly flips the matching tile to reveal it. Hint mode does this automatically after every first flip. Both are available in the game footer.

Pure browser

Zero dependencies. No server, no install, no accounts. One HTML file that runs offline in any modern browser including iOS Safari.

How to play

  • 01Configure the board. Go to the Board tab. Use the width and height scroll wheels to set the grid size. Hit the Launch game button when ready.
  • 02Flip a tile. Click any face-down tile to reveal its color and shape. The timer starts on your very first click.
  • 03Flip a second tile. Click another face-down tile. Each pair of flips counts as one move.
  • 04Match check. If both tiles share the same color AND shape they are a pair — they erupt in fireworks and spiral away, clearing the board permanently.
  • 05No match. If the tiles differ, both flip face-down again after a short delay. Try to remember their positions for later.
  • 06Hint. After flipping the first tile, press Hint in the footer to briefly reveal its matching pair. The hint resets after each use so it can be used repeatedly.
  • 07Hint mode. Toggle Hint mode to automatically reveal the matching tile every time you flip the first tile of a pair. Useful for learning the board.
  • 08Win condition. Clear all pairs from the board. Your final time and move count are displayed. Press Play again to reshuffle or Change board to adjust the grid size.

Colors & shapes

Each tile has a solid color background with a white shape at 60% surface coverage. The palette pairs each ROYGB primary with its white-tinted counterpart — easily distinguishable but clearly related. Colors are randomly selected each game; minimum colors used equals tiles ÷ 4 (rounded up).
